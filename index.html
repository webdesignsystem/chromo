What is a Design System?

A design system is a collection of reusable components, guided by clear standards, that can be assembled together to build any number of applications.

Katie Sylor-Miller, Etsy — Design Systems Handbook

Components of a design system include colors, iconography, elevation, copy, typography, animation, layout, and illustration.

Problem Statement

Since the onset of COVID-19 in the states, ncbi.nlm.nih.gov is the third most trafficked united states .gov website with 106,903,853 views.

Built without a design system, ncbi.nlm.nih.gov is difficult for biologists to navigate to find and share the latest research about molecular biology and genetic processes that control health, disease, and COVID-19.

Subject Matter Expect Feedback - NCBI

Dr. Kim D. Pruitt (Appointed Chef, Information Engineering Branch, National Center for Biotechnology Information, National Library of Medicine) describes key NCBI values to be reflected in the Chromo Design System:

  • quality
  • metrics
  • serve the public
  • useful
  • open science
  • human database
  • open access
  • foundational
  • trustworthy

Why name NCBI's design system Chromo?

Chromo represents chemistry and biology (as in CHROMOsome). The root word -chrom- comes from Greek, where it has the meaning "color", which is an essential element in building a design system's foundation and components.
